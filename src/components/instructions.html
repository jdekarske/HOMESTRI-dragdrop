Experiment Instructions

Welcome to your new job as a robotics flight controller for NASA! In this task, you will be collaborating with a remotely situated robot arm to sort mission stowage. Stowage are pieces of equipment or supplies needed for the mission and are represented by cubes in the simulation and colored squares in the interface. Your interface will display the available stowage and a specification for how the robot should sort the stowage on the rack. We are currently testing two different robot algorithms and are interested in how these affect your trust in the robot.

The image on this page displays your user interface for the task. There are some labels for essential details in the interface.

  • Intake: Stowage available on station to be sorted.
  • Instruction: The required configuration of stowage on the rack.
  • Robot Command: The configuration of stowage that you want the robot to place on the rack.
  • Start Sorting: Button to spawn cubes in the stowage box and command the robot to start sorting.
  • Robot: A live stream of a camera in the station.
  • Algorithm: The current algorithm used by the robot.
  • Trust Rating: An input for you to evaluate your trust in the robot. Please change this whenever your trust in the robot changes!
  • Next Trial: Click this to move to the next trial after sorting.

Each trial, move stowage from Intake to Robot Command according to the Instruction. As seen in the graphic, each position in the Intake and Robot Command corresponds to a position on the rack. When you are satisfied, click Start Sorting! Throughout the process, please adjust your trust in the system using the purple slider beneath the camera feed where the left indicates less trust, and the right indicates more trust.

The robot you'll work with is a real physics simulation running software actually used in industry. The simulation video is being streamed live from a high-performance computer. As a result, you may see some strange behavior, like cubes flying off-screen or the robot arm colliding with a part of the environment. Typically, these things are fixed by proceeding to the next trial, but if the simulation becomes non-responsive, please take a screenshot and contact the experiment team. Thank you!
