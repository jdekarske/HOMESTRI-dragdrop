Experiment Instructions

Welcome to your new job as a robotics flight controller for NASA! In this task, you will be collaborating with a remotely situated robot arm to sort mission stowage. Your interface will display the available stowage and a specification for how the robot should sort the stowage on the rack. We are currently testing two different robot algorithms and are interested in how these affect your trust in the robot.

The image on this page displays your user interface for the task. There are some call-outs for essential details in the interface. Consider the pieces of stowage (color blocks) in Intake and the required configuration shown in Instruction. Move stowage from Intake to Robot Command by clicking the stowage piece and the desired position. The grey blocks correspond to positions on the top and bottom of the rack, as indicated by the number labels. You should do your best to follow the assigned configuration! When the configuration is ready, click the Send Stowage button to drop the cubes in the stowage box, then the Start Sorting button to signal that you are ready for the robot to start sorting. Throughout the process, please adjust your trust in the system using the purple slider beneath the camera feed where the left indicates less trust, and the right indicates more trust.

The robot you'll work with is a real physics simulation running software actually used in industry. The simulation video is being streamed live from a high-performance computer. As a result, you may see some strange behavior, like cubes flying off-screen or the robot arm colliding with a part of the environment. Typically, these things are fixed by proceeding to the next trial, but if the simulation becomes non-responsive, please take a screenshot and contact the experiment team. Thank you!
